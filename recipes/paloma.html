Mexico: Grilled Grapefruit Paloma

Description

While many think of the margarita as the most traditional Mexican drink, the paloma is the real star of the show! Grilled grapefruit and lime take this traditional Mexican cocktail to the next level, giving it the perfect mix of sweet, salty, and smoky. Be sure to save all the juices accumulated while grilling to get the richest, smokiest flavor in your drink.

Recipes

  • 2 limes
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 1 red grapefruit, halved widthwise
  • ¼ cup kosher salt
  • 1 cup ice cubes, or as neede
  • 4 fluid ounces tequila
  • 2 tablespoons simple syrup, or to taste
  • 4 fluid ounces club soda

Steps

  1. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  2. Cut limes in half widthwise. Cut 1 half into 3 wheels.
  3. Pour sugar into a shallow bowl. Dip lime halves, 2 of the lime wheels, and grapefruit into sugar to coat thoroughly.
  4. Place limes and grapefruit cut-side down on the hot grill. Cook, turning lime wheels occasionally, until browned and grill marks form, 5 to 10 minutes. Transfer to a rimmed baking sheet to cool.
  5. Juice grilled lime halves and grapefruit into a small pitcher. Pour in any juices that accumulated on the baking sheet. Stir in tequila and simple syrup. Divide mixture between glasses and top off with club soda. Garnish with grilled lime wheels.
